Experimenting with primers, foiling, and blending

Testing different Primers with SeLR/Mehron foiled shadow

Products used:

  • Mixing Liquid | Mehron Makeup
  • Blue Marble SeLr Spray — Coast Fiber Tek
  • Mixing Medium Shine | MAC Cosmetics Canada - Official Site
  • Photo Finish Smooth & Blur Oil-Free Foundation Primer - Smashbox | Sephora

Starting with Ordinary silicone primer. I applied the primer to the top of my left and right arm. I then applied SeLR/Mehron Mixing liquid to my shadow brush to foil the shadow. I applied the shadow to the different squares using both tapping and dragging motions. I then applied SeLR/Mehron shadow on the lower side of my arms to compare as a baseline.

The ordinary primer combined with SeLR/Mehron seems to lead to piling. But only with the blue, non-shimmery tightly packed powder.

Next, I tried blending the squares to see how well foiled shadow would blend under different circumstances. Primer under the SeLR performed the best, but blending required a lot of force. This caused the blue pigment to fall off, leaving pigment behind only within the texture of the skin. Kinda looks like a finger print.

Next, I switched the primer to Smashbox silicone primer. I also wanted to test using concealer as an eyeshadow primer, so I placed white cream on the lower squares from the MUF HD flash palette. We can see that the piling is gone. both Primer+Mehron and Concealer+Mehron led to blotchy applications.

Bottom is concealer + SeLR

Bottom is concealer + Mehron

Another blending test. We can see that Primer+SeLR held really tightly to the pigment. Blending is possible but very difficult. Concealer+SeLR has a slightly nicer blend. Blending on Primer+Mehron just lead to the pigment being stripped off.

Next, I attempted to blend by applying some Mac Mixing Medium to the blending brush. Definitely helped move things around, Mehron seems to have dealt the best with being blended with the medium.
